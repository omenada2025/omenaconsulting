A simple operating guide for capturing weekly product, delivery, and design updates, then reviewing them in Dashboard, Executive Dashboard, and Source Reports.

What This Dashboard Does

The dashboard centralizes weekly updates so leaders can see health, risks, progress, product mix, blockers, wins, and next actions in one place.

Core interaction

Use filters to focus the view. Click status cards, then click project cards to reveal Wins, Blockers, and Next Action.

Dashboard Workflow

The dashboard is designed around a simple weekly reporting loop.

1

Add Status

Submit one product, delivery, or design update.

2

Review Dashboard

Check portfolio health, product mix, risks, wins, and next actions.

3

Present Executive View

Use the leadership-ready view for focused discussion.

4

Edit Source Reports

Correct submitted reports and keep the source data clean.

5

Save & Recalculate

Saved changes update dashboard calculations automatically.

Main Views

Dashboard

Working portfolio overview.

  • Filter by reporting week, type of product, role, and delivery health.
  • Review Tracks, At Risk, Product Mix, and Avg Progress.
  • Click Portfolio Health cards for inline details.

Executive Dashboard

Presentation-ready leadership view.

  • Click a health status to focus projects.
  • Click a project to show Wins, Blockers, and Next Action.
  • Review Executive Attention and Owner Performance.

Source Reports

Source-of-truth report list.

  • Filter by owner, type, reporting week, health, and role.
  • Click a card to show all details.
  • Edit the form and click Save Changes.

Field Guidance

Product, Feature, Or Workstream

Use a consistent, recognizable project name. Example: Surveyor System - Onboarding.

Owner

Enter the responsible person or team. Multiple owners are okay.

Type Of Product

Legacy for existing products. New product for new initiatives, modules, or builds.

Role

Use Product Manager for delivery/product updates and UI/UX for design/research/handoff updates.

Reporting Week

The week this update belongs to. This drives filtering.

Start Date / End Date

Use start date for when work began and end date for target close or delivery.

Progress

Use a percentage from 0 to 100.

Stage

Examples: Discovery, Work in Progress, Testing, Design Review, Dev Handoff, Release, Completed.

Priority

Use Normal, High, or Critical depending on urgency and visibility.

Delivery Health

On Track

Progress is healthy and no major action is needed.

In Progress

Work is active and moving through execution or review.

At Risk

Blocked, delayed, unclear, or needs leadership attention.

Paused

Work is not currently active.

Writing The Update

Good Summary

Say what changed this week. Keep it specific and factual. Avoid broad statements that do not explain progress.

Required Next Action

Always include a concrete next step. This makes the dashboard useful during follow-up meetings.

Win

The most important progress or achievement.

Blocker Or Risk

Anything that could delay delivery, design, review, feedback, or handoff.

Risk Impact

Low, Medium, or High based on how much the blocker affects delivery.

Time To Solve

Short, Medium, or Long based on expected effort or time needed.

Recommended Weekly Process

  1. Each owner submits or updates their status report for the reporting week.
  2. Omena Consulting leads review Source Reports for accuracy.
  3. Update health, blockers, and next actions before leadership review.
  4. Use Executive Dashboard during the presentation.
  5. Capture follow-up decisions as next actions for the following week.

Best Practices

Keep summaries short

Specific updates are easier to scan and present.

Always add Next Action

Every update should make the next step clear.

Mark true risks as At Risk

Do not hide risks inside the summary.

Use consistent project names

Consistent naming keeps weekly reports easier to compare.

Troubleshooting

  • Type of product does not appear saved: refresh the page and check Source Reports. If it still does not persist, Supabase may need the latest product_type schema migration.
  • No projects appear: change Reporting week, Type of product, Role / Portfolio view, or Delivery health.
  • Save Changes is not available: click a submitted report card first. The edit form opens on the right.

Quick Reminder

Submit one clear weekly update per workstream. Keep the Summary factual, the Blocker honest, and the Next Action concrete.
