1. What The Dashboard Does

The dashboard monitors product, feature, and UI/UX delivery. It shows what is on track, what is delayed, what is blocked, who owns the next action, and what leadership decisions are needed.

Monitor

Delivery health

Track products, workstreams, progress, dates, blockers, risks, and average delivery health.

Control

Actions and ownership

Convert blockers and delays into named actions with owners, target dates, and status.

Escalate

Leadership decisions

Highlight critical risks, delayed work, missing decisions, and items needing executive attention.

3. Weekly Status Workflow

Use this flow every week. The goal is not just to save a report; the goal is to make the report useful for decisions.

1

Open Status Center

Use Status Center to add a new report or edit an existing one.

2

Describe the work

Select product, feature/workstream, owner, participants, type, and role.

3

Update delivery health

Add reporting week, start/end date, baseline date, dependency, health, progress, stage, and milestone.

4

Make it actionable

Add summary, win, blocker, next action, action owner, target date, decision needed, and action status.

4. Add Status Field Guide

These fields are the minimum structure needed for dashboards, report analysis, owner feedback, and delay monitoring to work well.

Good status formula

What changed + why it matters + blocker + next owner + next date + decision needed.

A

Product context

Product, feature/workstream, owner, participants, product type, and role keep reporting clean and filterable.

B

Timing and delivery

Reporting week, start date, end date, baseline end date, dependency, health, progress, stage, and milestone power schedule classification.

C

Actionable narrative

Summary, win, blocker/risk, and next action explain what leadership should understand and what happens next.

D

Recovery controls

Delay root cause, date change reason, corrective action owner, action target date, decision needed, and action status turn delay reporting into a recovery workflow.

5. Schedule Rules

The app compares baseline and current end dates to classify schedule health.

Status Meaning What to do
Ahead of Schedule Delivery is more than 5% earlier than planned. Confirm scope is still complete and document the lesson learned.
On Time Delivery is within the expected schedule range. Keep next action measurable and continue monitoring.
Minor Delay Delivery is delayed up to 10%. Add root cause, recovery owner, and target date.
Major Delay Delivery is delayed more than 10%. Escalate, confirm decision owner, and review scope/date/resource trade-offs.
Date changes need a reason.

When the end date changes, use Date change reason to explain why the date moved and what was approved.

7. Status Center

Status Center combines submitted reports and editing in one workspace.

  • Select a report card on the left to edit it on the right.
  • Use filters to focus by owner, type of product, reporting week, health, and role.
  • Use History to review saved changes and audit events.
  • Export filtered data to CSV, Excel, or PDF.
  • Only delete reports when you are the owner or have the right permission.
  • After saving, refresh the report list and confirm the card reflects the update.

8. Report Analysis And Owner Feedback

Report Analysis

Find gaps and delivery risk

Checks missing context, unclear blockers, weak next actions, schedule risk, completeness, and escalation needs.

Owner Feedback

Coach each owner

Generates feedback by owner showing what they reported, what is unclear, what they should do next, and lessons learned.

Best feedback structure
  • What the owner reported.
  • What is missing or unclear.
  • What action the owner should take next.
  • What delivery risk needs escalation.
  • What lesson should be carried into the next week.

9. Action Items

Action Items turns reports into follow-up work.

Escalations

Critical priority, at-risk health, or major delay signals.

Blockers

Reported blockers or risks that need resolution.

Missing next action

Reports that need a clear next step.

Schedule recovery

Minor or major delay items requiring recovery planning.

Closed actions

Completed follow-ups that should be used as good examples.

Report quality

Reports missing context, owners, target dates, or decisions.

10. Best Practices

  • Use one clear owner per report.
  • Keep participants separate from the owner.
  • Do not leave next action blank.
  • If the end date changes, always explain the reason.
  • If the report is at risk, identify the decision needed.
  • Close actions only after the work is truly resolved.
  • Review Report Analysis before weekly leadership meetings.
  • Use Owner Feedback to coach each PM or UI/UX owner.
